eTrading Systems Training

Bridging the Critical Knowledge Gap in eTrading Eco-System

Understand the WHYs behind WHAT you do

What's the Gap

o   Fragmented understanding of eTrading systems across all roles & hierarchies.

o   Holistic understanding of Inter-System interactions remains elusive for majority of developers.

o   Developers don t understand The Big Picture and remain constrained to their immediate system while designing.

o   Technologists don t appreciate the Reasoning behind Regulations and treat compliance tasks as a boring, mandatory task.

o   Business and Technologists struggle to understand each other's concerns and constraints.

 

What's the Impact on Key Roles

Developers

  • Often work in silos with limited exposure to the "big picture," focusing only on their specific component and team's system.
  • They may not understand the impact of their design choices on downstream systems.
  • They may also lack awareness of how their work fits into the broader trading landscape, including regulatory frameworks and risk management.
  • This limited perspective can make them feel disconnected from the business, as they may not see how their work add value to business.
  • They often rely on a "system expert" who understands the interactions across systems, creating a significant risk if that expert leaves the firm.

 

Tech Leads & Architects

  • Struggle to provide strategic technical direction and foster effective cross-team collaboration.
  • Their teams' fragmented knowledge can lead to suboptimal architectural decisions and fragile systems.
  • They may push back on collaborative work with other teams because they lack a vision for systems beyond team level.
  • This often leads to suboptimal design choices and fragile systems prone to downtime and poor scalability as a group of systems.

 

 

Managers & Executives

  • A lack of deep understanding of trading systems can impact their ability to make informed decisions about resource allocation, project prioritization, and risk assessment.
  • This can lead to them struggling to evaluate design proposals, understand technical challenges, or champion long-term strategic initiatives.
  • This gap can lead to missed opportunities and trust deficit between leadership and engineering.

 

Business Stakeholders

  • Often lack a fundamental understanding of a system's capabilities and limitations, leading to misaligned expectations and friction over project schedules,
  • They may experience disconnects in conversations with tech teams and may not be able to effectively map their regulatory or trading strategy goals to technical feasibility.

 

 

The secret to innovation isn't always a new tool. Sometimes, it's a shared language.
